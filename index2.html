Hi, I am currently pursuing my Masters degree at MIT.
I have a rigourous background in working with data and algorithms to analyze transportation networks. Metropolitan scale transportation networks provide humongous and noisy data streams that either need to be processed in realtime for information provision or archived and analyzed later for modeling and policy analysis. Together with some amazing people, I have tackled each of these aspects with a variety of data sources and organizations in different network settings-
  • TimeGeo: A PNAS published patent-pending fine-scale urban mobility model. It generates individual mobility traces by tying together the inference of home and work activity locations from data, with the modeling of flexible activities in space and time. The model was built using cell phone data from over 2 Million residents of Boston.
  • Building Occupancy: Actionable insights are the foundation for meaningful change. Keeping this in mind, one of the objectives of my thesis is to create algorithms to distribute people in individual buildings and points of interest. One of the long term goal is to relate these to patterns of energy consumption and to weed out inefficiencies.
  • A Real-time Traffic Information System (RTIS) to provide dynamic traffic information on a 16km long Intelligent Transportation System study corridor in Chennai. First of its kind to do so for heterogeneous traffic lacking lane discipline
  • Smart Cards: Analysis of real-time and pseudo real time smart card data from the transit network of Brisbane, Gold Coast and Sunshine Coast for optimizing service performance and modeling traveler behavior. Also measured elasticities of passengers to service reliability using observed route choice.
  • Electronic Ticketing Machines: Analysis of travel patterns and revenue management system in Chennai to redefine fare slabs to promote long-distance travel.
  • Accident Prevention System: Built a prototype vehicle-mountable Accident Prevention System with a pulse monitor, speedometer, obstruction detection module and automatic steering in case of potential accidents
  • Urban Autonomous Delivery: Built a coffee delivery robot that was able to deliver more than 50 coffees to customers within 2 hours.
The details of some of these projects are provided below. I enjoy delivering high fidelity results and research in a fast paced environment with a motivated team.
Outside of research, I can be found working out (playing squash, swimming, hitting the gym or on long jogs) or playing with my Arduino/RaspberryPi.

Boston research in action

Research Projects

PNAS: Boston Cell Phone data

Using Call Detail Records, we have built high precision models for users which are subsequently used to simulate the mobility patterns of 4.5 Million residents of Boston. A paper detailing this was published in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Science (PNAS) on Aug 29, 2016.

CASPT: Smartcards in Brisbane

Smartcards (called GoCards) enjoy a high penetration rate in Brisbane. For my masters thesis, as a collaborative process between IIT-Madras and the University of Queensland, I used data from the 2 Million residents to build models for computing reliability and occupancy for the public transit system consisting of buses, ferries and trains. The results were presented in the Conference on Advanced Systems in Public Transit (CASPT)

GPS Probes and Video Cameras in Chennai

The Real-time Traffic Information System (RTIS) provides dynamic traffic information on a 16km long Intelligent Transportation System study corridor in Chennai. As a full time research assistant for 2.5 years, I used advanced technologies (GPS, video cameras, wireless communication, traffic models and algorithms) to provide realtime traffic information that is updated every minute.
