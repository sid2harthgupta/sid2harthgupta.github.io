A tale of (mainly) 3 cities-

1. Boston

Call Detail Records (CDRs) are fairly ubiquitous across the world. Historically these were discarded due to sheer volume and lack of mining techniques. I am currently working on using models derived from statistical physics to simulate trajectories of 4.5 million individuals in the Greater Boston area. Alternative data sources such as Wifi data from from Skyhook and surveys are used to bridge the knowledge gap in the CDRs. At the end of the project I hope to be able to predict building occupancy profiles and incorporate the impact of special events such as concerts and sporting events on the mobility patterns of a region.
A co-curricular objective I am actively pursuing at MIT is getting a full-stack grasp of data systems- from building IoT systems to data analysis and information provision. My team won the Disrupting Mobility Hackathon by building an Arduino based prototype of a 'mesh network navigation system' for damage appraisal and relief in case of disasters.
As part of a course at Media Labs, we built a coffee delivery robot and delivered 50 cups of coffee in 2.5 hours.



2. Brisbane (Australia)

Spatio-temporal Brisbane

Journeys can be considered multi-dimensional vectors in a spatial-tamporal space. These can be used to capture a multitude of phenomena occurring in cities from traveler perception to determination of inefficiencies.







Farecard based schedule matching and improvement

A task traditionally performed using AVL data transferred to AFC datasets, opening up a multitude of new opportunities for traveler and planner information

The transit network of Brisbane has a zone-based fare structure. Due to this, passengers need to tap-on and tap-off using their smartcards. This provides an almost complete universe of journeys performed on the transit network. I was interested in building a traveler information system using these data. Starting with an internship during which I focused on market segmentation based on individual behavior, I forged a collaboration that allowed me to analyze the data for my masters thesis. I built an information system to provide measures of service reliability and occupancy. I leveraged optimization algorithms and machine leadning for schedule matching, passenger segmentation and capturing traveler perception. I also used multinomial logit models to capture travelers' sensitivity to different service characteristics.



3. Chennai (India)

As a freshman at IIT-Madras, I was part of a team that built an Accident Prevention System (ToI News) consisting of 4 modules- a speed controller, an obstruction detector, overtaking feasibility checker and pulse monitor. This pushed me towards Intelligent transportation systems (ITS) and urban data analytics. I then used an image processing software to model traffic flow and to build incident detection applications. As part of the ITS Lab, I was part of the team that developed the first real-time information system (RTIS) for heterogenous traffic conditions using a live stream of GPS data.
